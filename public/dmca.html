Copyright infringement notification procedure.

Last updated: July 6, 2026

Reporting copyright infringement

IPScope respects intellectual property rights. If you believe content on our site infringes your copyright, send a DMCA notice to our designated agent with the following information:

  1. Your physical or electronic signature.
  2. Identification of the copyrighted work claimed to have been infringed.
  3. Identification of the material claimed to be infringing and information reasonably sufficient to locate it (URL).
  4. Your contact information (address, telephone, email).
  5. A statement that you have a good-faith belief that use of the material is not authorized by the copyright owner.
  6. A statement, under penalty of perjury, that the information in the notification is accurate and that you are authorized to act on behalf of the copyright owner.

DMCA agent

Email: legal@vinitk.dev
Subject line: "DMCA Takedown Request"

Counter-notification

If you believe material was removed in error, you may submit a counter-notification including your signature, identification of removed material, a statement under penalty of perjury that removal was mistaken, and consent to jurisdiction of the appropriate court.

Repeat infringers

We may terminate access for users who are repeat infringers where applicable.

Note on IP data

Geolocation results displayed by IPScope are derived from licensed third-party databases and public RDAP registry records. If you believe registry or database attribution is incorrect, contact the relevant registry or database provider; we will assist where technically feasible.
